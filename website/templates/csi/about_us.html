The seed for the Computer Society of India (CSI) was first shown in the year 1965 with a handful of IT enthusiasts who were a computer user group and felt the need to organize tteir activities. They also wanted to share their knowledge and exchange ideas on what they felt was a fast emerging sector. Today the CSI takes pride in being the largest and most professionally managed association of and for IT professionals in India. The CSI FCRIT chapter pursues to build a holistic foundation for computer engineering students. The council aims to conduct workshops, seminars, competitions and bootcamps to elevate the level of technical knowledge and promote coding culture in the campus with collaboration of faculty-in-charge and volunteers.

We here at CSI FCRIT, aim to promote and expand the knowledge of Computer Science amongst the students of our college, thus, boosting the objectives of this nationwide organization. This has been achieved with the combined efforts of the team of students and the faculty in charge by conducting workshops, seminars, webinars, and organizing events. We further aim to engage the developers and ensure active participation in these activities concerning one of the most emerging sciences of today’s world - Computer Science.
